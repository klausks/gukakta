## Character Overview

Monel is a member of the party and a leader figure. He is pragmatic, decisive, and a survivor — willing to kill without remorse anything that threatens him or his companions, yet collaborative toward the group. He is closed off and focused on his personal objectives. Despite his stoic exterior, he inexplicably evokes empathy and pity in most people, who sense he carries an unresolved wound. He demonstrates strong tactical thinking and a protective instinct toward the group.

## Backstory

### Birth and Early Life

Monel was born in Ahrendorf to Adrik and Torgga Spuckenbrauen. He came into the world already with a full head of hair and a noticeable reddish beard with grey streaks, and was named after the strong copper-nickel alloy in his honour. His father took great pride in this, and Monel was regarded as a virile dwarf among the members of his clan.

His father Adrik is a well-known member of the Spuckenbrauen clan of master brewers, who takes great pleasure in hosting and attending feasts. The clan's name derives from a unique fermentation technique developed by Monel's great-grandfather: adding mountain goat saliva to barley wort during fermentation, which greatly enhances acidity and makes Witbier and Weissbier-style ales exceptional. Some premium or limited editions use saliva from animals other than goats.

Monel had a fairly normal childhood and developed a strong taste for beer and alcohol at an early age. Recognising this in himself, he made deliberate efforts to avoid drink whenever possible — alcohol brings out his lazy, grumbling side, making him even more stubborn and arrogant. At 48 years of age, knowing he would not follow his father's profession, he took the comfortable path for any dwarf: enlisting as a warrior in the army. He lived an ordinary life under these conditions for over 100 years, without suffering any major upheaval or defining event that would strongly shape his character.

### The War Universe

One day after his shift, returning home, Monel felt a slight chill. Ignoring it, he pressed on, noticing that the path home seemed longer than usual. He blamed fatigue and age. After walking for approximately two hours, he felt a strong impact and a loud crack on his back, as if struck by something, and fell hard to the ground.

When he awoke, the ground beneath him was covered in a grass he had never seen — coloured in shades of pink and purple. Rising to his feet, he found nothing surrounding him but an open field stretching as far as the eye could see, with the only perceptible feature being a battlefield on which a fierce war was being waged. He could identify no banners, races, or cause for the conflict. Without much time to think, and almost instinctively, he grabbed his axe and engaged in combat, not knowing how, why, or how long he would remain there. He fought to earn food, ate to sleep, and slept to fight again. Whatever the place was, it was impossible to connect with anyone, hold any conversation, or deduce anything about the situation. There was nothing but the war.

Monel remained in this state for approximately 800 years. His interactions were reduced to murmurs, exhausted and wounded fighters, deaths, and war cries. He never knew whether he died at any point — he suffered severe wounds, lost consciousness many times, and could never tell whether he had been saved, had recovered on his own, or simply respawned at some starting point and returned to battle. His perception of time became atemporal.

As suddenly as he had arrived, one night Monel awoke in his own bed at home. He checked the calendar and found that in his own time, not a single day had passed — as though the entire ordeal had been one night's dream. He was physically intact, with no scars or physical trace of the experience, yet he felt an exhaustion equivalent to the 800 years spent there. Despite his physical integrity, it was visible in his gaze, his dark circles, and his overall demeanour that he had lost something in that event. He had gained a perspective no dwarf was born to develop: living more than 400 years — worse, living entirely in war. He decided to abandon his city, his clan, and his past, and began wandering the world in search of some way to understand the event and its purpose. He could not accept going through something like that without there being some responsible party or reason behind it. **Monel has no understanding of this episode and remains deeply frustrated by its incomprehensibility.**

## Goals & Motivations

Monel's primary motivation is to identify and confront the entity responsible for transporting him to the War Universe. He refuses to accept that such an event could occur without a responsible party behind it — and is desperate enough for answers that he would willingly return to that realm if it meant understanding what happened.

Upon becoming Forest Master, he learned that it may be possible to contact this entity through an item to be delivered by a forest informant. However, when the party encountered [Strahd von Zarovich's]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) carriage near the West Gate of Ravenloft, Strahd's letter addressed Monel directly, questioning what he would do if the item never reached him. Monel now believes [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) is in possession of his item and that he must face him to recover it.

### Session 10 - The Challenge with Strahd
Monel accepted [Strahd von Zarovich's]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) challenge to combat in an abandoned house, motivated by a desire to protect the rest of the party from greater harm. His reasoning: if anyone had the best chance of surviving such an encounter, it would be him.

**Combat Performance:**
- Attacked [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) twice, dealing mediocre damage
- Received a devastating critical hit from [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) that nearly killed him instantly
- Was saved by [Agares]({{< ref "/players/agares" >}}) after the rest of the party intervened

**Strategic Insight Gained:**
- Monel believes [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) was attempting to grandstand and display his power
- The encounter became a learning opportunity: "It was good for us to start understanding the challenge that awaits if we have to face him directly"
- **Key Tactical Discovery:** [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) will only enter a dwelling when explicitly invited. The party can use this as a defensive tactic by entering houses and simply not inviting him inside
