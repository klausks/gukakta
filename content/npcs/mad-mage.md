## Overview

A figure of unknown identity who appears to have extensive knowledge of [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) and the nature of Barovia's curse. Messages left by the Mage on stone cubes floating in the Ethereal Plane claim that Strahd himself drove them to madness. The Mage resists the label of "mad mage" and insists their name is unknown to anyone.

[Arabelle]({{< ref "/npcs/arabelle" >}}), the young Vistani daughter of [Louvash]({{< ref "/npcs/louvash" >}}), claimed to have had conversations with the Mad Mage while held captive near [Lake Zarovich]({{< ref "/locations/lake-zarovich" >}}). How the Mage contacted her — and what was discussed — remains unknown.

## Personality

Resistant to being called mad. Self-aware enough to anticipate the label, yet clearly operating outside normal bounds. Their writings suggest profound disillusionment and knowledge acquired at great personal cost.

## Goals and Motivations

Unknown. Their messages suggest awareness of Strahd's vulnerabilities, but whether they act on this knowledge — or are even capable of doing so — is unclear.

## Connections

- [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) — claims Strahd drove them to madness
- [Arabelle]({{< ref "/npcs/arabelle" >}}) — the Vistani girl claims to have spoken with the Mage during her captivity

## Key Events

### Session 25
Floating stone cubes inscribed with messages from the Mage were discovered in the Ethereal Plane near the mill. The inscriptions were written in Subcommon and Primordial. Key messages included:

> *"Time does not exist for those who are in Barovia. We are in the infinite shackles of Strahd's evil. He himself forgot everything he has ever done. This may be what will end him. The Mage went mad because of him."*

> *"Beware of the spiders."*

> *"Do not speak with the spirits."*

> *"The Mage — some call him the mad mage — but nobody knows my name."*

Appears in [Session 25]({{< ref "/sessions/session-25" >}}).
