## Background

The War with Duke Gundar is referenced in a letter attributed to [Hianag Darastrix]({{< ref "/npcs/hianag-darastrix" >}}), discovered by the party in the forest.

According to Hianag's account, after being grievously wounded fighting the risen skeletal remains of the Black Dragon Nithhogr Nightwing, he was saved by [Count Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}). Hianag states that Strahd's "rescue" led into centuries of servitude: he served under Strahd for **roughly 300 years**, including during Strahd's war against **Duke Gundar**, and traveled the continent under Strahd's banner killing creatures and men.

Hianag claims Strahd promised that one day he would be allowed to return to his own world and people — a promise Hianag implies was never kept. The letter is dated to **the year 650 in the Barovian Calendar**.

## Significance

This is one of the few direct pieces of testimony suggesting:

- [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) waged war beyond his immediate domain.
- Strahd maintained long-term agents or slaves, bound by promises of freedom.
- The party's recent encounters may be part of a broader pattern: Strahd's cruelty is not only physical — he uses hope and obligation as weapons.
