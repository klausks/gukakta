## Background

Lords of Barovia is a historical volume presented as **A Brief Historæ of the Succession of Lords**. The book traces the line of Barovia's rulers from **King Dostron** in year 1 through the rise of [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) in year 347.

Its front matter states that this copy is the property of [Castle Ravenloft]({{< ref "/locations/castle-ravenloft" >}}) and the Strahd Archives, with research completed in Avgust of year 348. It also presents itself as authenticated by the Cadian Court and the interim ministry of the Barovian Court.

## Significance

The book gives a ruler-by-ruler history of Barovia and includes an additional memorial accounting of royal and holy crypts beneath the castle.

Key historical claims in the volume include:

- **King Dostron** united Barovia, began Castle Barovia, and ruled through fear and respect.
- A secret place of Morninglord worship near [Krezk]({{< ref "/locations/krezk" >}}) was later fortified and turned into an abbey after Dostron's death.
- **Prince Ariel du Plumette** died before coronation after a failed attempt to fly from Castle Barovia.
- **Openhind Endorovich**, **Eisglaze Druf**, **Intree Katsky**, and **Strevoit Troitsky** each held power over Barovia during long transitional periods.
- **Falkon von Hapsburg** is described as the most loved of Barovia's rulers and is closely tied to Krezk, Tasha Petrovna, and the abbey there.
- During the Terg war, **Lord Argynvost and the knights of the Silver Dragon** fought beside Barovian forces, tying this history to [Lords of the Silver Dragon]({{< ref "/lore/lords-of-the-silver-dragon" >}}).
- The book records [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) as born in year 306 and as the conqueror who retook Barovia in year 347 after the deaths of his father Barov and Count von Hapsburg.

As a court-authenticated history held in Strahd's own archives, the volume is valuable both as a chronology and as an example of how Barovia's ruling history is officially presented.
