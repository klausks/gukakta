## Background

During [Session 25]({{< ref "/sessions/session-25" >}}), the party discovered stone cubes floating in the Ethereal Plane near the mill. The cubes bore inscriptions written in Subcommon and Primordial, signed only by **"the Mage."** The author refers to themselves in the third person in some messages and the first person in others — seemingly aware that others might call them "the mad mage," but resistant to the label, insisting that **nobody knows their name**.

The messages suggest the author has extensive knowledge of [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) and the nature of Barovia's curse, and claims that [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) himself drove the Mage to madness.

## The Inscriptions

The following messages were found on the floating stone cubes:

> *"Time does not exist for those who are in Barovia. We are in the infinite shackles of Strahd's evil. He himself forgot everything he has ever done. This may be what will end him. The Mage went mad because of him."*

> *"Beware of the spiders."*

> *"Do not speak with the spirits."*

> *"The Mage — some call him the mad mage — but nobody knows my name."*

## Significance

The messages imply that [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) has forgotten his own past actions, and that this forgetfulness may be a vulnerability. The Mage appears to have had direct knowledge of [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) and suffered for it. Their identity, current whereabouts, and fate are entirely unknown.
