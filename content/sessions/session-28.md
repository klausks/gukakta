## Summary

The party awakens to find the wolves' bodies gone and their tracks deliberately obscured by human bootprints. Following the trail, [Jimmy Slit]({{< ref "/players/jimmy-slit" >}}) scouts ahead while maintaining a mental link with [Pepino Falstaff]({{< ref "/players/pepino-falstaff" >}}). The group meets three friendly wolf-hunters — [Vasili von Holtz]({{< ref "/npcs/vasili-von-holtz" >}}), [Yevgeni]({{< ref "/npcs/yevgeni" >}}), and [Szoldar]({{< ref "/npcs/szoldar" >}}) — who guide them safely to [Vallaki]({{< ref "/locations/vallaki" >}}) and warn them about the city's strict rules, including a taboo against speaking or carrying writings of [Strahd's]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) name. Before entering the gates, the party dyes [Ireena Kolyana's]({{< ref "/npcs/ireena-kolyana" >}}) hair and conceals anything that could connect her — or the party — to Strahd. At the gate, guards test the group for demonic taint and issue official visitor documents, along with a list of laws and obligations within the city.

## Events

### The Wolves Are Gone

The group wakes under light drizzle. The wolves' bodies are missing. [Monel]({{< ref "/players/monel" >}}) identifies subtle traces suggesting someone tried to erase the trail; the remaining evidence points to human boot soles. Jimmy searches for the lost ring from the prior day but finds nothing.

### Scouting the Trail

The party follows the bootprints. Jimmy moves ahead in stealth while Pepino keeps a mental connection to relay information. [Agares]({{< ref "/players/agares" >}}) rides his summoned horse; [Igga Bevneros]({{< ref "/players/igga-bevneros" >}}) casts Aid on himself, Jimmy, and Monel.

Along the way, Jimmy discovers a small roadside chapel that appears to have seen recent violence — arrows litter the area, and markings on the altar suggest an icon of a sun was once present, bearing the words "Lord of the Morning." He recovers three arrows in good condition.

Jimmy also spots an animal trap in the path — a bear trap — and marks it so the group can avoid it.

### Ireena's Dream

Ireena describes a disturbing dream: a huge creature holding many dolls made in her likeness — cloth dolls with her face and hair. In the dream, the creature shifted in form, sometimes a giant demon, sometimes a strong man.

### The Wolf-Hunters

The bootprints end where a wolf is caught in a trap. Three men drop from a tree and introduce themselves as wolf-hunters: Vasili von Holtz, Yevgeni, and Szoldar. They keep their weapons lowered and claim they mean no harm; they explain wolf meat and hides are traded in the city, and Yevgeni and Szoldar wear heavy wolf-leather clothing.

When asked about the missing ring, Vasili says they did not find it and does not appear to be lying.

Vasili demonstrates a pouch large enough to stow a whole wolf and says he obtained it from [Esdras]({{< ref "/npcs/esdras" >}}). The hunters invite the party to accompany them to Vallaki. Notably, the mists seem to part for Vasili as they travel.

At one point, a bright ball of light begins following the group. Vasili warns the party never to follow such a creature — it is hostile — and they endure its pursuit for a time before it finally withdraws.

### Advice for Vallaki

On the road, Vasili offers guidance about Vallaki:

- The party should present themselves properly and meet [Baron Vargas Vallakovich]({{< ref "/npcs/baron-vallakovich" >}}), who is known for his festivals.
- For Ireena's protection, they should seek St. Andral's Church.
- The Blue Water Inn is the city's principal inn.
- It is forbidden to mention Strahd's name or carry documents that include it.

Vasili notes that Vargas's father was more severe than Vargas, and says the baron's wife, [Baroness Lydia Petrovna]({{< ref "/npcs/lydia-petrovna" >}}), is a woman of faith devoted to the "Lord of the Morning." He also mentions the family has children and suggests the party will likely encounter them during a festival.

Vasili remarks that it is obvious the party is hiding something about Ireena, and says he "expected more" of them, given that they have survived this long.

### Preparing Ireena and Hiding Strahd's Name

Before the gate, the party separates from the hunters and makes their preparations: Ireena's hair is dyed and her appearance adjusted, and the group hides or discards any letters or materials that mention Strahd. Pepino disguises the tome to avoid suspicion.

Ireena speaks of her possible curse, says she was adopted, and shares fragments of childhood memories. Monel observes that whenever Ireena sleeps, disaster seems to follow, and asks her to always speak up when she feels unwell.

### The Gates of Vallaki

At the gates of Vallaki, guards subject the party to tests to prove they are not demons: they spill water and require each person to step into the puddle, then press a silver coin to each forehead. Nothing happens.

A third guard named [Izek]({{< ref "/npcs/izek" >}}) appears, notable for a huge red arm that looks unnatural.

The gates open. The party is required to declare their names, ages, and the weapons they carry. They receive official visitor documents and are informed of the city's laws:

1. None shall speak the name of the Devil or carry written documentation bearing his name.
2. None shall slander the Baron or the Festivals; written material that depicts them negatively will be rejected and dealt with.
3. All must participate in preparing festivals.
4. All must attend festivals.
5. None shall harm a Vallakian.
6. None shall steal from Vallakians or their property.
7. None shall deface or damage public or private property.

Inside the city, the party resolves to open [Ismark's]({{< ref "/npcs/ismark" >}}) letter in a safe location and expects to receive 50 gold per person.

## Decisions & Consequences

- The party concealed Ireena's identity and hid or discarded documents referencing Strahd before entering Vallaki.
- The party accepted guidance from Vasili von Holtz and his hunters, arriving safely at the city gates.
- The party received official visitor papers and is now subject to Vallaki's laws and festival obligations.
