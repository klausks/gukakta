## Summary

[Igga Bevneros]({{< ref "/players/igga-bevneros" >}}) and [Monel]({{< ref "/players/monel" >}}) wake up disoriented inside [Castle Ravenloft]({{< ref "/locations/castle-ravenloft" >}}), finding themselves at an elaborate banquet. [Strahd von Zarovich]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) joins them at the dinner table and requests that the party obtain the green gem from the winery for him. He grants each of them one question and allows them to explore the first floor of the castle until midnight. Igga reunites briefly with [Swigas]({{< ref "/npcs/swigas" >}}), who appears to be unharmed, and the party discovers two mirror-hidden secret entrances in the dining room.

## Key Events

### Waking at the Banquet

The pair awoke disoriented in the castle's dining hall, faced with a grand banquet. [Strahd]({{< ref "/npcs/strahd-von-zarovich" >}}) was seated at a pipe organ decorated with human skulls and bones, playing an absurdly melancholic piece. He then rose and welcomed them:

> "Welcome to Castle Ravenloft. I hope your visit is comfortable."

### Strahd's Request

Over dinner, Strahd expressed his desire for the party to obtain the **green gem** for him — the same gem recovered with the druid's staff at the [Wizard of Wines Winery]({{< ref "/locations/wizard-of-wines-winery" >}}).

When Monel asked why Strahd did not simply retrieve it himself, Strahd replied that he had learned over time that certain things work better when not forced. He referenced Ireena as an example — he could have taken her by force already, but chooses not to. He sometimes leaves such tasks to others, allowing each party to play their positive and negative role in how things unfold.

### Swigas' Appearance

Igga asked to see Swigas. Strahd had her brought in, escorted by [Rahadin]({{< ref "/npcs/rahadin" >}}), confirmed here as a trusted confidant of Strahd. Swigas appeared to be in good health and indicated she was fine. Notably, despite being described as a captive, she gave the impression of being there at least partly by her own choice.

Monel had no clock and asked Swigas for help. She offered him her pocket watch — and was promptly slapped on the hand by Rahadin for doing so.

### One Question Each

Strahd offered each of them one question:

- **Igga** asked why the people of Barovia are so sad. Strahd answered that Barovia reflects the nature of its leader — and that he himself is a somber and miserable person.
- **Monel** asked what the green gem is for. Strahd said it is precious to him and of little importance to the party. He claimed it is needed to **resurrect his mother**, who died in an accident. The party sensed he appeared to be telling the truth.

### Freedom to Explore

Strahd permitted the party to freely explore the **first floor** of the castle until midnight. The second floor and the crypt were declared off limits.

### Discoveries in the Castle

- **Dining Hall:** Two mirrors in the dining room appear to function as hidden secret entrances. Their destinations are unknown, but they seem to be interconnected. The room also houses the pipe organ decorated with skulls and bones.
- **Main Entrance Hall:** Dragon statues line the hall, and a stone carving of Strahd looking downward is carved into the walls. No secret mechanisms were identified, and no magical investigation was conducted.
- **Atmosphere:** The entire castle is described as intensely cold.

## Decisions & Consequences

- The party now knows Strahd wants the green gem and has offered it to him as a possible negotiating point.
- Swigas is confirmed alive and relatively unharmed at Castle Ravenloft.
- Rahadin is confirmed as physically present at the castle and as Strahd's trusted attendant.
- The party chose not to explore where the mirror passages lead.

## Loose Threads

- What is behind the two mirrors in the dining hall?
- Is Strahd's claim about his mother true? What would resurrection of his mother entail?
- What is on the second floor and in the crypt?
- How did Igga and Monel get to Castle Ravenloft, and how do they return to the others?
- Why does Swigas appear to be at the castle willingly despite being a captive?
