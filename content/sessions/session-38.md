## Summary

A light rain begins to fall over [Vallaki]({{< ref "/locations/vallaki" >}}) as the party juggles competing priorities: planning for a mission to [Krezk]({{< ref "/locations/krezk" >}}), acquiring knowledge about Barovia, and managing the growing complications at [Wachterhaus]({{< ref "/locations/wachterhaus" >}}).

At [Androm]({{< ref "/npcs/androm" >}})’s bookshop, the party reviews compiled books (including a bestiary-style compilation and a war-history compilation), hears that research compilations take days to prepare, and notes several scrolls for sale—one identified as *Speak with Dead*, another described as a disguise spell, and a third that remains unidentified. A monster-hunting book connected to [Rudolph van Richten]({{< ref "/npcs/rudolph-van-richten" >}}) is also noted for containing an attached note.

Meanwhile, [Jimmy Slit]({{< ref "/players/jimmy-slit" >}}) visits St. Andral’s Church to speak with [Father Lucian Petrovich]({{< ref "/npcs/lucian-petrovich" >}}) about the demon situation and learns what religious services and rites the priest can provide.

Back at Wachterhaus, [Halik]({{< ref "/npcs/halik" >}}) receives [Igga]({{< ref "/players/igga-bevneros" >}}) and [Agares]({{< ref "/players/agares" >}}), settles arrangements for Agares’ horse, and escorts them through a spotless, perfumed home staffed by servants such as [Amalthia]({{< ref "/npcs/amalthia" >}}), [Madalena]({{< ref "/npcs/madalena" >}}), and the cook [David]({{< ref "/npcs/david" >}}). When [Fiona Wachter]({{< ref "/npcs/fiona-wachter" >}}) finally presents her daughter, [Stella Wachter]({{< ref "/npcs/stella-wachter" >}}), the party confirms that Stella is affected by necromancy and enchantment magic—and her reaction to the mention of [Viktor Vallakovich]({{< ref "/npcs/viktor-vallakovich" >}}) suggests a link to whatever happened to her.

The evening ends with a tense social game of masks and roles that exposes Fiona’s lethal pragmatism, and with the party deciding to pursue more information—about Stella, about the Baron’s son, and about the captured imp.

## Events

### Rain and Planning
A fine rain starts to fall. Jimmy briefs the group again on the Krezk mission, while Igga and Agares attempt to plan next steps.

### Bookshop Options and Scrolls
At Androm’s shop, the party notes several options and prices:

- **Compilation: Creatures and Legends of Barovia** — 80 gp
- **Compilation: War Regions and History** — 60 gp
- **Rare-book option (sale only)** — 420 gp

They are told a custom compilation may take **2–3 days** to prepare, and that they may take notes and carry only a few books.

The shop also has **three scrolls** available:

- *Speak with Dead* — 150 gp
- A scroll described as magic to appear as another person — 50 gp
- One unidentified scroll

### Jimmy at St. Andral’s Church
Jimmy goes to St. Andral’s Church and speaks with Father Lucian about the demon. Jimmy also learns Lucian can provide holy rites and services, including the ability to make holy water and perform spells such as *Gentle Repose*, *Iron Vision*, and *Purify Water*.

### Arrival at Wachterhaus
Igga and Agares are received by Halik, who says Fiona is resting or praying. Halik arranges for Agares’ horse to be taken to a transport stable, then seats them in a clean, perfumed room with cocktails while they wait.

Halik offers to store Agares’ belongings; Agares insists on keeping them close.

### Guest Rooms and Household Staff
Halik introduces Amalthia (a maid) and shows Agares a room said to belong to [Nikolai Wachter]({{< ref "/npcs/nikolai-wachter-ii" >}}). Amalthia comments on Agares’ armor and is reprimanded.

Halik then shows Igga his room, and later the location where the captured “diabrete” is being kept. A common name is given for the imp: **Majesto**.

Agares notes they previously tortured the imp for information and then healed it again, but still did not learn enough.

The imp is heard repeating: *"My darv, my darv"* (Infernal for "my lord").

### Stella’s Condition
Later, Fiona presents Stella and asks the party to see what they can do. Agares greets Stella by name and tries to draw out a response; Stella shows a degree of awareness.

Igga uses *Detect Magic* and identifies **necromancy and enchantment** affecting Stella, with no other magic apparent in the room.

The household reports Stella was with the Baron’s son when this happened, but the circumstances are unknown. Agares asks how the household learned of her condition; Fiona says her servants informed her.

When Agares asks if Viktor did this to her, Stella reacts abruptly and becomes increasingly restless. Halik moves to take her back to her room, but Igga offers to soothe her with calm music on the lyre.

### Masks and Questions
After dinner, Fiona asks Halik to bring games. Halik brings three masks and a deck. Fiona gives Agares the most exotic mask.

Roles are drawn:

- Igga draws **the Conspirator**
- Agares draws **the Traitor**
- Fiona draws **the Guilty**

Through a series of pointed questions, Fiona reveals a harsh worldview:

- If a friend conspires against her, she would feel betrayed, and she would choose **death** as the response—sometimes as “the best solution.”
- When asked whether she would decide a friend’s death herself, she answers **yes**.

Fiona says she likes the game because it relieves the political pressure she lives under.

## Decisions & Consequences

- The party continues pursuing solutions for Stella, while recognizing they need more information.
- Igga offers music as a calming approach when Stella becomes agitated.
- Agares and Igga plan to coordinate information-gathering about Stella, the Baron’s son, and the captured imp.

## Loose Threads

- What is the full nature of the necromancy and enchantment affecting Stella?
- What can the captured imp (“Majesto”) reveal if properly interrogated?
- What does the note connected to Rudolph van Richten’s monster book say?
